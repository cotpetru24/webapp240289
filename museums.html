Coventry Cathedral

Explore the ruins of original St Michael’s Cathedral destroyed in the Coventry Blitz during WWII, which are standing proudly next to the new cathedral, the magnificent 'Coffin of the Jewels'. The New Cathedral displays works by the great artist of the 1950s and his 60s. Internationally recognized as a beacon of hope, Coventry Cathedral embodies the spirit of peace and reconciliation in a truly magnificent setting. You can climb the cathedral tower to enjoy the best views of Coventry and the surrounding area. Climb all 180+ steps before grabbing a bite to eat at the Tower Gift Shop. You'll find lightning-themed items, retro toys, candies, generic cathedral souvenirs, and even squirrel-shaped items.

Transport Museum

For a truly unique day out for the whole family, look no further than the Coventry Transport Museum. This extensive museum in Coventry city centre contains the world's largest collection of publicly owned British road vehicles. Discover the stories behind the people who made Coventry the centre of the world's automobile and bicycle industry, and get an up-close look at the actual cars that shaped some of history's most pivotal moments. The museum is designed to travel through time. Wander through a 19th-century city to discover the first bicycles, carriages and automobiles, explore the first car factories and take your breath away as you watch war-torn at the Blitz Experience. Face off against the world's fastest car, the Thrust SSC, while navigating the city's biggest industrial ups and downs. No other place in the world offers such a unique experience for the whole family.
